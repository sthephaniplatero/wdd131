El Salvador

El Salvador, the smallest country in Central America, is known for its scenic volcanoes, Pacific beaches, and rich culture. With a population of about 6.5 million, its economy relies on agriculture, remittances, and tourism. San Salvador, the capital, blends colonial history with modern development. The country adopted the U.S. dollar in 2001 and made Bitcoin legal tender in 2021. Famous for its traditional pupusas and sites like Joya de Cerén, a UNESCO site, El Salvador attracts visitors worldwide. Despite past challenges, including a civil war, the nation’s resilience and cultural pride continue to drive progress and innovation.
